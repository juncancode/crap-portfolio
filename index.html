Marie Kondo

Character Design Sheet

Background

Turning Marie Kondo into a cartoon

Tools Used: Photoshop | Pencil | Wacom Tablet

The basic character design sheet includes a turnaround which showcases various angles and poses along with various emotions through a story. Japanese organizing consultant, Marie Kondo, inspired me to create a cartoon version of her.

Process

Step 1 - Drawing

After drawing the turnaround -- front, side, and back views -- on a paper, I imported scanned version into Photoshop in a letter sized document (RGB with 72 DPI for Web). I used her real photos and guides to match the overall look of the character.

Step 2 - Coloring

The step-by-step process begins with a drawing clean lineart that utilizes both pressure size brush and Pen Tool. I then color the base skin by choosing color palettes/swatches and clip it to the base layer using and add additional lightings using Pen Tool, Blending Mode, and Vector Mask for a precise and flexible outcome.

Step 3 - Expressions

As many says "Story is king", I drew various poses with different angles and emotions. For example, "Spark Joy" is a famous quote of Marie Kondo whose signature pose is pointing up with her index.

Reflections

Result

It was a pleasure getting noticed and liked by Marie Kondo herself who also left a comment on my Instagram.

What I learned

I have become more comfortable drawing folds and clothing lines through drawing the skirt. I tried not to overdraw the lines to complicate the wrinkles.

Next Steps

I look forward to drawing more people and hopefully some post my artwork on their Instagram page to get exposed by more audience.
